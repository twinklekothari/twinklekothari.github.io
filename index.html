About Me

Hi, I'm Twinkle Kothari, a Ph.D. student in Aerospace Engineering at the University of Texas at Arlington. My research dives deep into the world of beam lattice metamaterials, where I combine experimental mechanics with computational modeling to unlock new possibilities in structural design.

I have hands-on expertise in finite element analysis (FEA) using Abaqus, mechanical testing through MTS systems, and automation through Python scripting. My work is driven by a passion for innovation, precision, and pushing the boundaries of aerospace materials and structures.

Beyond research, I have contributed as a Teaching Assistant, guiding students through core aerospace engineering principles, and worked on projects in collaboration with leading institutions like Purdue University and NASA-funded initiatives. I'm always eager to explore new research opportunities, collaborate across disciplines, and contribute to the advancement of aerospace science.
